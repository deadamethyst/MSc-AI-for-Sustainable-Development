\# Applied Artificial Intelligence



This folder contains all weekly labs completed for the \*\*Applied Artificial Intelligence\*\* module of the MSc in AI for Sustainable Development.  

The labs focus on practical applications of AI, including classical techniques, modern machine learning approaches, and hybrid systems, applied to real-world problems.



\## Module Information



\- \*\*Faculty:\*\* Faculty of Engineering Sciences  

\- \*\*Teaching Department:\*\* Computer Science  



\### Aims



This module aims to equip students with:



\- Fundamental concepts and techniques of AI to solve complex real-world problems.  

\- Knowledge and tools to tackle new AI problems in different fields.  

\- Ability to propose and design AI solutions for a range of domain-specific applications.  

\- Skills to be effective team players in interdisciplinary research groups developing and applying AI systems.



\### Intended Learning Outcomes



Upon successful completion, students will be able to:



\- Demonstrate understanding of fundamental AI concepts.  

\- Understand classical and modern AI approaches.  

\- Recognize real-world applications for AI modeling.  

\- Implement and critically evaluate state-of-the-art AI techniques across domains.



\### Indicative Content



Topics typically covered include:



\- Introduction to AI approaches and deployment to solve complex real-world problems.  

\- Theory and practice of classical AI: problem representation, search-based AI, knowledge representation, logic-based information technologies, reasoning and planning strategies.  

\- Introduction to hybrid approaches combining classical AI with modern machine learning, such as symbolic neural networks.
