# Accountable, Transparent, and Responsible Artificial Intelligence (COMP0195)

This folder contains coursework and lab work completed for the **Accountable, Transparent, and Responsible Artificial Intelligence** module as part of the MSc in Artificial Intelligence for Sustainable Development.

The module focuses on the ethical, societal, and technical implications of deploying AI systems, with particular emphasis on fairness, transparency, accountability, safety, and policy-relevant AI design.

---

## Module Information

- **Faculty:** Faculty of Engineering Sciences  
- **Teaching Department:** Computer Science  

---

## Aims

Responsible AI combines principles, practices, and tools that enable the development and deployment of AI systems in an ethical, transparent, secure, and accountable manner.

This module aims to:

- Develop a broad understanding of the implications of AI technologies in real-world contexts.  
- Equip ML and AI practitioners with tools to build robust and trustworthy AI systems.  
- Provide applied exposure to fundamental concepts and current research trends in responsible AI.  

---

## Intended Learning Outcomes

On successful completion of the module, students are able to:

- Understand the principles and theoretical foundations of transparent, accountable, and human-centred AI systems.  
- Critically discuss the societal and real-world impacts of artificial intelligence technologies.  
- Evaluate machine learning models in terms of fairness, bias, robustness, and accountability.  
- Develop and validate algorithmic practices for responsible and ethical AI deployment.  

---

## Indicative Content

Topics covered in this module include:

- Human-centred AI system design  
- Algorithmic fairness and bias mitigation  
- Interpretable and explainable machine learning  
- Privacy-preserving and secure AI algorithms  
- AI ethics, governance, and policymaking
